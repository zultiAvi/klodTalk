KlodTalk — Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 20, 2026

Introduction

KlodTalk is a voice-first interface for communicating with AI agents running on your own local network. This policy explains how the app handles your data.

Microphone & Speech Recognition

KlodTalk requests the RECORD_AUDIO (microphone) permission solely to power Android's built-in speech-to-text engine (SpeechRecognizer API). Specifically:

  • No audio is recorded or stored. The microphone stream is consumed entirely by Android's on-device speech recognizer.
  • No audio is transmitted. Neither raw audio nor any audio-derived data is sent to any server, third party, or external service.
  • Only the resulting transcribed text is used by the app.

Network Communication

The transcribed text is sent over a WebSocket connection to a server on your own local network, configured entirely by you (IP address and port). KlodTalk does not connect to any external or cloud-based servers. No data leaves your local network through the app.

Data Collection

KlodTalk does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. In particular:

  • No analytics or usage tracking.
  • No advertising or ad-related SDKs.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect user data.
  • No crash reporting sent to external services.

Connection settings (server address, username) are stored locally on your device using Android's SharedPreferences and never leave the device.

Third-Party Services

KlodTalk does not integrate with any third-party services. The only network communication is the WebSocket connection to your self-hosted local server.

Children's Privacy

KlodTalk is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.
